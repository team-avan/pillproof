How it works

A simple workflow with controls to detect invalid tests, built for non-experts

1

Swipe a small sample

Gently scrape a tiny amount of powder and swipe across multiple reagent lanes to increase coverage.

2

Dip and develop

Dip the card edge into water. Capillary flow carries sample through lanes, forming a color “barcode.”

3

Scan with the app

The app frames the card, normalizes lighting, and measures lane colors objectively.

4

Get a clear output

Returns Detected, Not detected*, or Inconclusive with safety reminders and resources.

*“Not detected” means not detected at the test threshold and does not prove a pill is safe. PillProof is designed to reduce interpretation errors and increase sampling coverage, not to replace laboratory confirmation.

Why it’s different

Existing tools either require expensive equipment or rely on users interpreting faint lines. PillProof’s advantage is multi-lane coverage and software interpretation.

PillProof advantages
  • Multi-lane swiping to increase sample coverage and reduce missed “hot spots.”
  • App-assisted reading (lane-by-lane measurement) to reduce human interpretation errors.
  • Controls to flag invalid tests (poor flow, lighting issues, incomplete development).
  • Designed for B2B2C distribution through campuses, public health, and harm reduction organizations.
  • Privacy-first scanning, with no account needed and opt-in aggregated reporting.
How we position it

PillProof is a community screening platform that is between simple fentanyl strips and complex laboratory confirmation. It is designed to be low-cost enough for bulk distribution while adding the interpretability and program reporting institutions need.

Competes with: fentanyl test strips and implicit safety Complements: labs and professional spectrometers

Validation plan

We are high school students and cannot legally or safely handle controlled substances. Our plan builds technical confidence now and moves to controlled lab validation with an approved partner.

Milestones
M1: Card flow and lane readability tests (safe dyes/surrogates) M2: App robustness (lighting, phones, angles) and invalid-test detection M3: Controlled lab calibration (LOD, cross-reactivity, repeatability) M4: Pilot with an institution (for usability, adoption, and outcomes) M5: Scale manufacturing
We will publish pilot outcomes and limitations transparently with partners.

FAQ

Does “Not detected” mean the pill is safe?

No. It means the target signal was not detected at the test threshold. Sampling, adulterant distribution, and other factors can affect results. PillProof always includes safety reminders and encourages informed decisions.

How can PillProof help my organization?

PillProof is a screening tool intended to make harm-reduction decisions more practical. We anticipate high user adaption with low costs to improve overall community health.

Will PillProof store personal data?

The scanning experience is designed to work without accounts and to process results on-device. If program reporting is enabled, the only data recorded is the purchasing organization and the count of “Detected” results. No personal or location data is recorded.

What substances can it detect?

PillProof is being designed to screen for suspected fentanyl and other common adulterants using multi-lane chemistry. Final detection scope depends on lab calibration, validation, and partner requirements.
